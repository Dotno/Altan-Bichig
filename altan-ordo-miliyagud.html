Miliyaġud-un Takilġ-a.

Annointment of the Golden Palace-Tent (i.e., Horde)

By:

Encoded by Pount, Dashdorjiin Dotno

This text is a part of the critical edition of the Cult of Chinggis Khan transmitted texts, edited by Dotno D. Pount as a part of a PhD dissertation (pending completion and defense), under the supervision of Prof. Chris Atwood, at the University of Pennsylvania, department of East Asian Languages and Civilizations.

Oġonos Manuscript. f48 - f50. The MS was kept in the family of Prof. Oġonos Čoġtu, and was published as Yang, Haiying. “Kinsho” kenkyū e no josetsu. Suita-shi: Kokuritsu Minzokugaku Hakubutsukan, 1998.

Eldengdei Manuscript. f95 to f99.

Sayinǰirġal and Šaraldai edition. AOT p. 70-

Notes: The ending in L title indicates this prayer is the "Altan Ordo-yin Miliyaġud", and is found in O, but not in T. This helps distinguish two separate bodies of texts all related to the relics called "miliyaġud". From context of this prayer, it appears that the miliyaġud are the straps of the ordo. The editors of S note (p. 70) that this version is in the Engke Amuġulang 22 (1722) copy, the year in colophons found in T as well... The note on p.75 says that the Darqad yamutad Bousuwar, Čenggel, Batuwǰir etc., said that this prayer is not the Altan Ordon-u Miliyaġud-un Irügel. They suspected this to be a part of the Gürbelǰin Ġou-a Qatun-u čomčog-un miliyaġud. As this is a part of the 1722 ms, S and Š argues that the other Miliyaġud-un Irügel in #8 is likely older than this, as this has many references to Buddhism. This note also mentions the existence of woodblock printed Altan Bičig., presumably also containing versions of this prayer. I have not found those yet.
